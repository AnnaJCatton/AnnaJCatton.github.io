Full Ahead! - Co-op twin stick vehicle shooter

My final year project, a co-op game designed to test teamwork by putting two players in control of half of a tank each. The goal from the start was to have players yelling at one another lovingly - a goal I achieved in testing.
All of the models, assets, and gameplay - with the exception of a pack of foliage models and a few mp3 files - were designed and made by me. I am particularly proud of the tank asset and the speed-of-sound delay built into the shell's whistle audio, which is entirely synthesized, and it's explosion noise.

The game went through many iterations, starting as a four-player experience wherein each player would be on their mobile devices, essentially playing minigames in real time to control a single tank on a larger shared screen. As the project developed, it became clear that this would lead to some players 'waiting their turn' in a non-turn based game, so I moved to a two-player model, which was far more engaging. I would later go on to develop a system for real-time mobile web control in the style of my original idea in my Emergent Games Technologies Module.

Tools: Unreal Engine 5, Blender, Adobe Creative Suite

Time: 2 semesters, concurrent with another module throughout.

Greatest challenge: Creating the game entirely from scratch required me to learn several tools I had not yet used, such as the Metasounds editor, and the Chaos Wheeled Vehicle systems.

Context: Entirely created solo for my Final Year Project module, for which I recieved a first-equivalent grade. (70%)

Note: Requires a windows PC and two gamepads.

Pizza Time! - Procedural House Generator

A tool to generate houses from a procedural pack quickly and easily. Created to ease the workload on a small level design team who were required, thanks to our brief of a pizza delivery game, to build a town in a short period.

Although I had had time to make a prototype version of the tool using my own assets, I only recieved the final assets from our 3d art team within a week of the final deadline - and as they had not made clear specifications as to how they would create the assets, the tool had to be reworked in a matter of days so that the level designers could use it for the final build.

Tools: Unreal Engine 5, Blender, Adobe Creative Suite

Time: 1 semester, concurrent with another module and while acting as producer.

Greatest challenge: Tight turnaround between recieving the final modular pack and the final deadline. (<1 week)

Context: Created during my Senior Collaborative Games Development module, as a piece of a final product created by a team of ~20.

About me:

Hi, I'm Anna!
I am a 22 year-old prospective technical designer who is currently looking for work. Although I have specialized in bridging the gap between design and production through rapid prototyping, I have developed knowledge in a large number of areas and have experience in fields from pure design to technical art.

Before achieving a 2:1 in Gameplay Design and Production from Staffordshire University, my background was a mix of computer science and graphic design - both subjects I chose with the aims of becoming a better game designer. My first ever games were 1-minute text-based 'adventures' written in batch files for the windows console - but I've come a long way since then!

The games I play most often tend to be more unique indie games - titles like Noita, Townscaper, and Kerbal Space Program have all informed my experience as a designer. Outside of that, I also often play sandbox games and first-person or vehicle shooters.

When I'm not making (or playing) video games, I tend to be found playing or listening to music, playing board games and TTRPGs, and occasionally I find time to bake!
